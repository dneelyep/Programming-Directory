Kenny

Instrument: Electric Guitar/Vocals

Kenny Harrington was born on December 12, 1956 in Marion County, Kentucky. After finishing school in 1975, the leader of a popular area group called Cheyenne spotted him in a local music store. He was then hired as a roadie and eventually as a guitarist. They did one LP of top 40 covers. Kenny’s next group, called Landslide, played in the late 70's and featured Larry McDonald on vocals/drums and Bill Wilson on bass/vocals. Bill Lester came later on guitar/vocals, eventually being replaced by Richard Patterson on guitar/vocals. This group lasted about 4 years. Richard’s then wife Marty was added to the lineup and the band’s name was changed to FeverCat. Then in 1986, the rock band XYZ was formed with Richard Patterson on guitar/vocals/keyboards, Paul Moore on bass/vocals, Byron Cravens on drums and Rebecca White on vocals. In 1988, Kenny joined a group from Lexington called Phoenix who were the house band at the Continental Inn. This band travelled twenty six weeks a year, although the gig ended in 1990. Then in the late 90's, he went to work for Crooked Creek along with, again, Larry McDonald and Richard Patterson, which lasted 3 years. Kenny’s current project started from a jam at a friend's party and has brought them to form Shakedown. Chris Neel is on bass/vocals, Daniel Neel is on drums, and Kenny is playing guitar/guitar synth. They are on a mission to play classic rock covers, new country and to entertain all age groups.

Kenny is married, has four children, and has two grandchildren. He is the oldest of nine children. His father was in the military and his family travelled a lot. He got his first guitar when he was 13 and played in his first band at age 18.

Kenny’s musical influences include Billy Gibbons, Joe Satriani, Johnny Winter, Floyd Radford, Gary Rossington, Rod Price, Eric Clapton, Keith Richards, John Fogerty, and more.
